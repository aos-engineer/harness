# Navigator — Initial Position
## Meridian Analytics Council | API Platform vs. SaaS Depth
## Session: 2026-03-24 | Round 1

## Scratch Pad (Working Intelligence)

**Market window assessment:**
- API-first analytics is not a new category — it is becoming table-stakes infrastructure for the Series B+ segment
- Amplitude launched its API suite in 2021; Mixpanel's Data Export API is mature; Rockerbox (closest ICP match) has had REST APIs since 2023
- The window is not "opening" — it is already past peak for the enterprise-API category. We are entering late, not early.
- However: causal inference via API is genuinely uncrowded. Nobody has a programmable attribution engine with causal modeling. That is the specific position, not "analytics API" generically.

**Competitive signals:**
- Amplitude raised $400M+ and is building toward a "DXI platform." Their API is a feature, not a business model.
- Rockerbox is the most dangerous competitor for this ICP. Attribution-focused, similar buyer, APIs already available. If they ship causal inference or partner with a modeling layer, our window closes.
- No competitor has shipped an AI-assisted insights layer in this category yet. That is an open positioning lane.

**Distribution reality check:**
- Self-serve: CAC $4,800, LTV:CAC 4.7x, payback 14 months — this is a working machine
- Enterprise: 0 closed deals, no enterprise AE, no SE. Distribution does not exist.
- The two at-risk customers ($280K ARR) represent a retention problem, not a distribution proof point. Retaining them via API ≠ a repeatable enterprise motion.

**Timing windows:**
- AI-assisted insights: Window is open NOW. No competitor has shipped this in the attribution space. Estimated open: 12–18 months before a funded competitor replicates.
- Programmable attribution API (niche): Window is narrowing. 18–24 months before Rockerbox or an upstart closes it.
- Full enterprise API platform (broad): Window is largely closed. We would be entering a mature, well-funded competitive set late.

---

## Position: **Option B with a Scoped Export API Rider**

I oppose Option A as scoped. I support Option B as the primary investment. I advocate for a formally scoped, deliberately constrained export API as a rider — not a full platform.

Here is my market reasoning.

---

## 1. Market Context

The framing of this decision as "API platform vs. SaaS depth" obscures the real market question: *which positioning do we own, and is the distribution infrastructure in place to monetize it?*

Meridian's current position is strong and underappreciated internally. We have a 4.7x LTV:CAC ratio, 4-day median time-to-first-attribution, a 4.7/5 G2 score, and a proprietary causal inference engine that no direct competitor has matched. That is a differentiated position in the self-serve growth analytics category. It is working. The competitive clock on it, however, is ticking — Amplitude and Mixpanel are both moving toward attribution, and we have no moat if we stall feature velocity for 6 months.

The enterprise segment is not a market we are *losing* — it is a market we have never entered. Those are different problems. Losing a market you occupy requires a defensive response. Failing to enter a market you've never competed in requires a *distribution strategy* before it requires a *product strategy*. We have neither an enterprise AE, a solutions engineer, nor a formalized enterprise pricing model. A $420K API build without the GTM infrastructure to sell it into enterprise is not a strategy — it is an engineering project.

---

## 2. Positioning Recommendation

**What we should be in the buyer's mind:** *"The attribution platform that actually shows you causality, not correlation — and it takes 4 days to see your first result."*

That position is ours today. No competitor can say it honestly. Option B compounds it. Option A dilutes it by shifting our identity toward "analytics API platform" — a category where we are late, outgunned, and without distribution.

The AI-assisted insights layer is a *category-creating move* within our existing position. If we ship a layer that surfaces causal attribution insights in plain language — "your paid search is cannibalizing your organic, here's the revenue impact" — we are not competing with Amplitude on features. We are redefining what "understanding your attribution" means for the VP of Growth buyer. That is a positioning advantage that is structurally difficult to replicate quickly, because it requires the causal inference engine underneath it. Competitors cannot copy the surface without copying the foundation.

**The export API rider:** A formally scoped, read-only data export API (not a full platform) can be delivered in 6–8 weeks with 1–1.5 engineers. It would satisfy the two at-risk customers' primary use case (embedding attribution data in their internal dashboards), reduce churn risk without the full Option A commitment, and allow us to test whether enterprise buyers actually convert when given programmatic access. It is a market intelligence instrument, not a full platform bet.

---

## 3. Timing Window

| Initiative | Window Status | Latest Viable Entry | Risk if We Wait |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI-assisted insights layer | **Open — uncrowded** | Q4 2026 | Competitor with LLM integration ships first; we lose differentiation claim |
| Causal inference export API | **Narrowing** | Q2 2027 | Rockerbox or new entrant ships programmable causal attribution |
| Full enterprise API platform | **Past peak** | Already late | Entering mature category without distribution; high cost, low probability |

The AI insights layer window is open *now* and we have the underlying asset (causal engine) that makes our version defensible. Option B captures this window. Option A misses it entirely — we spend 6 months building infrastructure for a market we cannot yet sell into, while a competitor ships the insights layer and takes our positioning lane.

---

## 4. Distribution Path

**Option B distribution:** Self-serve growth motion → product-led expansion → AI insights layer as upgrade trigger. The distribution path exists, is proven (187 customers, CAC improving), and the AI layer creates a natural expansion revenue event without adding GTM headcount.

**Option A distribution:** Cold enterprise outbound → needs enterprise AE + SE we don't have → long sales cycles → no reference customers → POC demands that exceed our support capacity. This distribution path does not exist. Building the product does not create the channel.

I want to say this plainly: **a product without a distribution path is a prototype.** The two at-risk customers are not a distribution proof — they are retention pressure. Retaining them validates that some existing customers want API access. It does not validate that we can acquire *new* enterprise customers via an API motion. Those are different questions, and we are conflating them.

---

## 5. Competitive Response Risk

**If we choose Option B:**
- Rockerbox or a new entrant builds a causal inference API — this is the real competitive risk, and it materializes in 18–24 months regardless of our decision today. Option A does not foreclose this risk; it just moves us from "no API" to "early API."
- Amplitude continues feature velocity in attribution — their distribution advantage means feature parity does not help us. Our moat must be the insight quality, not the feature list.
- **Mitigation:** The export API rider signals to the market and to at-risk customers that programmable access is on the roadmap, buying us 1–2 renewal cycles to prove enterprise demand before committing to the full platform.

**If we choose Option A:**
- Amplitude or Mixpanel ships an AI insights layer while we are building API infrastructure — we have ceded our open positioning lane
- We ship the API platform in Q1 2027 with no enterprise sales motion and discover that the three declined prospects have moved to alternatives
- The two at-risk customers renew, but we have no second wave of enterprise buyers to justify the $420K investment
- **This is the scenario I am most worried about:** we win the retention battle and lose the positioning war.

---

## Single Most Important Consideration

> **We are being asked to build distribution we do not have, to serve a segment we have never sold to, with a product motion we cannot staff — while leaving open the one positioning lane that is uncrowded and defensible today.**

The $280K ARR retention pressure is real. But a decision made under retention pressure, without a distribution strategy for the segment we're buying into, is not a market strategy — it is a panic response dressed as a roadmap. The export API rider addresses the retention risk at a fraction of the cost and commitment. Option B with the rider is the move that preserves our position, captures an open window, and tests enterprise demand before betting the engineering cycle on it.
